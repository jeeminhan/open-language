She kept the real language alive.

A small thing that grows with your language.

Concept 04 · The Cold Case

She's working the case from the other side.

A Signal-style frame: your tutor is a detective from another time, reaching you via a phone line that shouldn't exist. Together you work unsolved cases — witness statements, coded diaries, old transcripts, newsreel footage. She teaches you language because you need it to read the evidence.

How to pick.

All three share the same engine — same adaptive tutor, same SRS queue, same three call agendas. The only difference is who answers the phone.

01 · The Censored World   widest appeal. Premise does the work — every user is inside the story just by existing. Reads as literary-atmospheric, not mission-driven. Best fit for the level mechanic as world-reveal.
02 · The Secret Channel   strongest character pull. Specific person you're calling, specific stakes. Higher intensity, higher risk of feeling heavy day after day.
04 · The Cold Case   tightest forward pull. Signal-style reveals — each level is a new lead, new keyword, new Sora "archive clip." Best fit for the level mechanic as case progression. Risk: needs case content per arc (writing investment).
03 · The Growing Companion   easiest sell to casual learners. Also the only one that contradicts the existing brief. Low-key Duolingo-adjacent — use only if repositioning the product.
What the level chip means.
Everyone starts at level 1. Leveling up is driven by engagement, not mastery — so a beginner and a fluent speaker at "Level 5" see the same lore, the same Sora clip, the same unlocked keyword. The tutor is still adaptive in call; the narrative is shared.

Each level unlocks: one lore fragment (narrated), one in-world keyword (say it to the tutor for a special response), one archival Sora clip (styled as leaked footage, not cinema).

The archive strip at the bottom of Home is the quiet entry point. Tap it → full archive drawer of everything unlocked so far. See LEVEL_BIBLE.md for the Level 1–10 outline.
