# Level Bible — Chapter 1 (Levels 1–10)

Outline for the level-unlock mechanic. Each level unlocks three payloads:

1. **Lore fragment** — 20–40s of narrated context the tutor delivers in-character the first time the user reaches that level. Voice only, in the target language with optional subtitles.
2. **In-world keyword** — a word or phrase. The user says it to the tutor on any future call and the tutor has a scripted special response (defined per keyword). This is diegetic — not a UI button.
3. **Archival Sora clip** — a short (10–20s) atmospheric video, styled as leaked footage / archival material / surveillance capture. Grainy, muted, 4:3 or VHS-grade. Stamped 機密 / CLASSIFIED where appropriate. Never polished cinema.

## What triggers level-up

Engagement-driven, not skill-driven. A working default:

- Call for **15+ minutes total** since the last level up, **across at least 2 sessions**
- OR **10 words passed** in the SRS drill

Either triggers the next level. Not a grind — a pacing mechanism.

Everyone starts at Level 1 regardless of language ability. The tutor is adaptive inside the call; the narrative is the same for everyone.

## Chapter 1 — "Before Common"

Setting: the Censored World (Concept 01). Yuki for EN→JP; Sam for KR→EN. Parallel content, same world. The examples below are for Yuki — Sam's chapter is the same beats with English as the forbidden register.

---

### Level 1 — The Opening

- **Lore:** "Common was rolled out thirty years ago. You don't remember before. Neither do most people. I do. That's why we're talking."
- **Keyword:** `はじめまして` (hajimemashite) — "the way you greeted someone when you wanted them to know you meant it." Saying it to Yuki triggers a 20-second response where she teaches the three registers of first meetings Common collapsed into one.
- **Sora clip:** grainy archival footage, a classroom from before — children reciting poetry in unison, audio warped and slowed, timestamp 昭和63年.

### Level 2 — The First Archive

- **Lore:** how the Authority built Common from seven "safe" registers. What was pruned: metaphor, literary verbs, regional dialect, most kanji compounds above JLPT N3.
- **Keyword:** `言の葉` (kotonoha, "leaves of speech") — older, poetic word for language itself. Saying it unlocks a teaching about metaphor as forbidden register.
- **Sora clip:** security camera footage, 3am, a library's rare-books room — books being quietly boxed and loaded onto an unmarked truck.

### Level 3 — Yuki's Pen Name

- **Lore:** she used to write. Not under her real name. The pen name she used is lost; she'll tell you only if you ask.
- **Keyword:** `小野` (Ono) — a surname. If the user says it on any future call, Yuki pauses, then tells them something she shouldn't: her pen name was Ono Sanae. She published two books before Common.
- **Sora clip:** warmly-lit 1990s home video of a study — a hand moving across a page with a fountain pen, no face shown, a radio playing Miles Davis.

### Level 4 — What You're Not Allowed to Miss

- **Lore:** formal and casual registers were collapsed into one "neutral" mode. Apologies are especially damaged. Here's what was lost.
- **Keyword:** `ごめんなさい` (gomennasai) — the full formal apology. Saying it to Yuki triggers her to teach the difference between this, `すみません`, `申し訳ない`, and when each was correct.
- **Sora clip:** 4:3 home video grade, two people bowing in the rain to each other outside a train station, timestamp 平成元年.

### Level 5 — The First Line

- **Lore:** Yuki's second book had a famous first sentence. She recites it to you. It was quoted in a court transcript that is still public.
- **Keyword:** `雨が降っていた` ("it was raining") — the first words of her banned book. The tutor treats this as a recognition phrase on future calls: if the user opens a call with these words, Yuki responds in kind and unlocks a secondary lore scrap.
- **Sora clip:** overhead shot of a ransacked apartment, pages strewn, a single book spine with the title visible for a beat.

### Level 6 — The Ledger

- **Lore:** somewhere, there's a list of words the Authority has "retired." You are now keeping a private one with Yuki. You are on page two.
- **Keyword:** `懐かしい` (natsukashii) — no exact Common translation, roughly "the warmth of remembering something lost." One of the first retired words. Saying it adds it to the in-world ledger.
- **Sora clip:** a handwritten list being photographed in secret on film, shutter sound audible, words unfocused but visible.

### Level 7 — The Second Voice

- **Lore:** Yuki mentions, carefully, that sometimes there is a second voice on the line. Not the Authority. Someone else. She doesn't know who.
- **Keyword:** `もう一人` (mou hitori, "one more person") — the user can now ask about the second voice. Yuki deflects twice, then in Level 9 she tells you more.
- **Sora clip:** static-laced shortwave radio frequencies, a distant voice half-audible in a language not obviously Japanese.

### Level 8 — The Archive Room

- **Lore:** there's a physical archive that predates Common. It still exists, in a district nobody visits. Yuki has never been, but she knows the door.
- **Keyword:** `中野区立図書館旧館` (Nakano-ku former municipal archive) — saying the full name of the place unlocks Yuki's monologue about what might still be inside it.
- **Sora clip:** an overhead surveillance drone shot of a disused municipal building at dusk, windows dark except one on the third floor.

### Level 9 — A Recording

- **Lore:** Yuki has a recording of her old teacher's voice. He was one of the first people arrested. She plays you thirty seconds of it.
- **Keyword:** `先生` (sensei) — in the context of this level, saying it unlocks Yuki's response in her teacher's accent (an archaic Kansai register). The user now learns one phrase *in his voice* before it reverts to Yuki.
- **Sora clip:** an audio waveform on an old cassette player, reel turning, the spooled tape catching warm light from a window.

### Level 10 — The Proper Name

- **Lore:** what Yuki is doing with you has a word in the old language. It describes the act of passing a language across an unsafe channel. She teaches it to you as a chapter-break.
- **Keyword:** `言伝 (kotozute)` — literally "word-passing." From this call on, the word lives in the ledger as the *name of what you two are doing*. Saying it triggers the first cross-chapter hint: "there are more of us."
- **Sora clip:** the street outside Yuki's room at dawn. Empty. For the first time in the chapter, it feels hopeful.

## How this adapts to other concepts

| Concept | "Level" becomes | "Lore" becomes | "Keyword" becomes | "Sora clip" becomes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **01 Censored World** | world-reveal depth | a piece of pre-Common history | a retired word | archival / surveillance footage |
| **02 Secret Channel** | letters exchanged | a piece of Yuki's past / her book | a coded phrase she uses | a smuggled photo, a prison corridor |
| **04 Cold Case** | leads uncovered | a piece of case backstory | a name, a place, a dialect phrase | "recovered" security footage, witness tape |
| **03 Growing Companion** | growth stages | what the creature now understands about the world | a word it now "knows" | the companion's dream visuals |

Chapter 1 is designed for concept **01 (Censored World)** but the levels map cleanly onto **04 (Cold Case)** — substitute Yuki's literary backstory for a specific case file, each level is a new piece of evidence, each keyword is case-specific. Writing investment roughly doubles for Concept 04 because evidence content is particular to the case.

## Production cost

For Chapter 1 (10 levels):

- 10 × lore fragments (~30s each) = ~5 min of tutor-voice script per language, per concept
- 10 × keyword triggers (scripted response, ~15s each) = ~2.5 min of scripted response per language
- 10 × Sora prompts (one-time generation, human color-grade pass) = ~10 × 20s video
- One archive-drawer UI surface (overlay showing everything unlocked)

Estimated solo-founder effort: **one focused week of content work** + **one week of Sora iteration** to get the aesthetic right (archival/leaked, not cinematic).

## Post-Chapter-1

End Level 10 on a cliffhanger. Do not ship Chapter 2 until there's signal from Chapter 1. Users who finish Chapter 1 are the ones worth building Chapter 2 for; they also tell you what worked.
